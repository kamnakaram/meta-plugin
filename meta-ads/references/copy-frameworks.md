# Meta Ad Copy Frameworks

> Meta-only copy reference — used by meta-create, meta-analyze, and meta-plan

## Overview

| Framework           | Expansion                           | Best For              | Placement Fit |
|---------------------|-------------------------------------|-----------------------|---------------|
| AIDA                | Attention, Interest, Desire, Action | Cold audiences        | Feed, Reels, Stories |
| PAS                 | Problem, Agitate, Solution          | Pain-point products   | Feed, retargeting |
| BAB                 | Before, After, Bridge               | Transformation offers | Reels, Stories, Feed |
| 4P                  | Promise, Picture, Proof, Push       | High-ticket / considered | Feed, retargeting |
| FAB                 | Features, Advantages, Benefits      | Technical products    | Feed, catalog |
| Star-Story-Solution | Star, Story, Solution               | Brand storytelling / UGC | Reels, Stories, Feed |

All templates target Meta character limits: primary text front-loads the hook
before the ~125-char "…more" fold; headline ≤40 chars; Reels/Stories primary ~72 chars.

## AIDA

**Steps:** Attention > Interest > Desire > Action

- **Best for:** Cold audiences, awareness campaigns, product launches
- **Placement fit:** Feed, Reels, Stories

**Meta templates:**
- Primary text (~125 chars): `[Attention hook]. [Interest detail]. [Desire benefit]. [Action CTA].`
- Headline (≤40 chars): `[Desire phrase] [CTA verb]`
- Reels/Stories primary (~72 chars): `[Attention hook] — [Desire benefit]`

**E-commerce example:** "Tired of dull skin? Our vitamin C serum brightens in 7 days. Join 50K happy customers. Shop now."
**SaaS example:** "Spending hours on reports? Automate analytics in minutes. See 3x faster insights. Start free trial."

- DO: Lead with a pattern interrupt; include a specific number or stat
- DO: End with a single clear CTA
- DONT: Stack multiple CTAs; DONT bury the hook after the first sentence

## PAS

**Steps:** Problem > Agitate > Solution

- **Best for:** Pain-point products, problem-aware audiences, retargeting
- **Placement fit:** Feed, retargeting

**Meta templates:**
- Primary text (~125 chars): `[Problem statement]. [Agitate consequence]. [Solution with brand].`
- Headline (≤40 chars): `[Solution phrase] [CTA]`
- Reels/Stories primary (~72 chars): `[Problem] → [Solution with brand]`

**E-commerce example:** "Back pain from your desk? It only gets worse without support. Our ergonomic chair fixes posture in a week. Order today."
**SaaS example:** "Leads falling through the cracks? Every lost follow-up costs revenue. Our CRM auto-sequences every lead. Try free."

- DO: Make the agitation specific and relatable; present the solution as relief
- DONT: Over-dramatize (triggers disapprovals) or use policy-violating fear language

## BAB

**Steps:** Before > After > Bridge

- **Best for:** Transformation offers, coaching, fitness, lifestyle products
- **Placement fit:** Reels, Stories, Feed

**Meta templates:**
- Primary text (~125 chars): `[Before state]. [After state]. [Bridge: how the product gets you there].`
- Headline (≤40 chars): `[After state] starts here`
- Reels/Stories primary (~72 chars): `[Before] → [After] with [Brand]`

**E-commerce example:** "Struggling with frizzy hair every morning. Now: salon-smooth in 5 minutes. The silk pillowcase that changed everything."
**SaaS example:** "Before: 4 hours building reports. After: dashboards in 10 minutes. Bridge: our AI analytics platform. Start free."

- DO: Make the contrast vivid and specific; keep the bridge concise
- DONT: Make unrealistic before/after claims; DONT skip the bridge

## 4P

**Steps:** Promise > Picture > Proof > Push

- **Best for:** High-ticket items, premium services, considered purchases
- **Placement fit:** Feed, retargeting

**Meta templates:**
- Primary text (~125 chars): `[Promise bold claim]. [Picture outcome]. [Proof stat]. [Push CTA].`
- Headline (≤40 chars): `[Promise] [Proof number]`
- Reels/Stories primary (~72 chars): `[Promise] — [Proof stat]`

**E-commerce example:** "Cut energy bills by 40%. Imagine comfortable rooms year-round. 12,000 homes upgraded. Get your free quote."
**SaaS example:** "Double pipeline velocity. Picture your team closing 2x deals monthly. Trusted by 500 B2B companies. Book a demo."

- DO: Back the promise with verifiable, specific proof
- DONT: Make promises you can't substantiate; DONT use vague proof

## FAB

**Steps:** Features > Advantages > Benefits

- **Best for:** Technical products, comparison shoppers
- **Placement fit:** Feed, catalog / DPA

**Meta templates:**
- Primary text (~125 chars): `[Feature spec]. [Advantage over alternatives]. [Benefit to user]. [CTA].`
- Headline (≤40 chars): `[Feature] = [Benefit]`
- Reels/Stories primary (~72 chars): `[Feature] → [Benefit]`

**E-commerce example:** "256GB storage, 12-hour battery. Outperforms competitors at half the price. Work all day, uninterrupted. Buy now."
**SaaS example:** "SOC 2 certified, 99.99% uptime. Zero migration downtime vs. legacy tools. Ship faster with confidence. Start trial."

- DO: Lead with the most differentiating feature; translate every feature into a benefit
- DONT: List features without outcomes; DONT use jargon the audience won't know

## Star-Story-Solution

**Steps:** Star (hero) > Story (narrative arc) > Solution (product reveal)

- **Best for:** Brand storytelling, emotional campaigns, UGC-style content
- **Placement fit:** Reels, Stories, Feed

**Meta templates:**
- Primary text (~125 chars): `[Star intro]. [Story challenge]. [Solution: product saves the day]. [CTA].`
- Headline (≤40 chars): `[Star]'s secret to [outcome]`
- Reels/Stories primary (~72 chars): `Meet [Star]. [Solution with brand]`

**E-commerce example:** "Meet Sarah, a busy mom of three. Mornings were chaos until she found our 5-minute meal prep kit. Now breakfast is handled. Try it."
**SaaS example:** "Meet DevOps lead Marcus. Deployments took his team 6 hours. With our CI/CD platform, they ship in 20 minutes. See how."

- DO: Make the star relatable; keep the arc tight (one challenge, one resolution)
- DONT: Make the star feel like a paid actor; DONT let the story bury the product

## Framework Selection Guide

**Cold audiences (unaware):**
- Awareness: AIDA or Star-Story-Solution
- Consideration: BAB or PAS
- Conversion: AIDA with a strong CTA

**Warm audiences (engaged, not converted):**
- Consideration: PAS or 4P
- Conversion: FAB or BAB
- Retargeting: PAS with urgency

**Hot audiences (past customers, high intent):**
- Upsell: FAB or 4P
- Retention: Star-Story-Solution
- Win-back: BAB with a new offer

When in doubt, start with PAS for pain-aware audiences or AIDA for broad reach, and
test two frameworks against each other in a Meta Experiments A/B split before scaling.
Under Andromeda, treat each framework as a *distinct concept*, not a variant.
